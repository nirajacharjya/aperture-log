01 Introduction

Your privacy matters. This Privacy Policy explains what information may be collected when you visit Aperture Log, how it's used, stored, and protected, and the choices you have regarding your personal information.

By accessing or using this website, you agree to the practices described here. Aperture Log is a personal blog and portfolio created to share articles, projects, photography, tutorials, technology, travel experiences, and educational content.

02 Information We Collect

What's collected depends on how you interact with the website.

Information you voluntarily provide

When you use the contact form, leave a comment, subscribe to a future newsletter, or email me directly, you may provide:

  • Name and email address
  • Username or profile photo, if applicable
  • Your message, and anything else you choose to share

I only collect information that you voluntarily submit.

Information collected automatically

Like most modern websites, Aperture Log automatically collects some technical information to improve the site — browser and device type, approximate location, pages visited, time on page, referring site, and session duration. This is collected in aggregate and generally isn't used to personally identify visitors.

03 Cookies

Aperture Log uses cookies and similar technologies to remember preferences, improve performance, analyze visitor behavior, and measure traffic.

You can disable cookies through your browser settings. Some features may not work correctly if you do.

04 Google Analytics

This website uses Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with it — pages visited, time on page, device and browser info, region, and traffic sources. Google Analytics uses cookies to generate anonymous statistical reports. For details, review Google's own Privacy Policy.

05 Google Search Console

Search Console is used to monitor performance in Google Search — indexing issues, search queries, and visibility. It does not collect personal information for marketing purposes.

06 User Accounts

In the future, Aperture Log may allow visitors to create accounts using Firebase Authentication, which may collect a name, email address, authentication identifier, and profile image. This is used only to provide account-related features.

Passwords are never stored directly by this website — authentication is securely managed by Firebase.

07 Comments

If commenting is enabled, submitted information may include your name, username, comment, profile picture, and timestamp. Comments may be publicly visible, and spam, abusive, or inappropriate comments may be removed without notice.

08 Contact Form

The Contact page collects your name, email address, subject, and message, used solely to respond to your inquiry. Messages may be securely stored using Firebase.

Your information is never sold or shared for advertising purposes.

09 Future Newsletter

Aperture Log may introduce a newsletter in the future. If you subscribe, your email will only be used for new articles, site updates, and important announcements. You can unsubscribe at any time — I will never send spam or sell your email address.

10 Artificial Intelligence

Some articles or content on this website may be created, edited, or improved with the assistance of AI tools. Every piece is reviewed, edited, and approved before publication — AI is used as a productivity aid, not a replacement for human review.

Articles on Aperture Log may link to external websites. Once you leave this site, I'm not responsible for the content, privacy practices, or policies of third-party websites. Please review their privacy policies separately.

13 Data Security

Reasonable technical and organizational measures are used to protect your information, including secure HTTPS connections, Firebase Authentication, secure database rules, access restrictions, and regular security updates.

That said, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure. While I strive to protect your information, I can't guarantee absolute security.

14 Your Rights

Depending on your country and applicable privacy laws, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal information
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information
  • Withdraw consent, where applicable
  • Request details about how your data is stored

To exercise any of these rights, reach out through the Contact page.

15 Children's Privacy

Aperture Log is not intended for children under the age of 13, and I do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has submitted personal information through this website, please contact me right away so it can be addressed.

16 International Visitors

Aperture Log welcomes visitors from around the world. By using this website, you understand that your information may be processed and stored in accordance with the laws governing this website.
